chan chan

Chan Chan is an ancient archaeological site located in the Moche Valley on the north coast of Peru, near the modern city of Trujillo. It was the capital of the Chimú civilization, which flourished from around 850 AD until its conquest by the Inca Empire around 1470 AD.

History :

The Chimú civilization emerged around the 9th century AD in the Moche Valley region of northern Peru. Initially, it was a small kingdom, but it gradually expanded its influence through conquest and alliances with neighboring societies

By the 11th century, the Chimú capital, Chan Chan, began to take shape as a monumental urban center. Its construction was likely initiated by the Chimú ruler Tacaynamo. Over the following centuries, successive Chimú rulers expanded and embellished Chan Chan, turning it into one of the largest and most impressive cities of its time. Chan Chan was meticulously planned, with a grid-like layout consisting of nine large compounds known as "citadels." Each citadel was surrounded by high walls and contained palaces, temples, warehouses, and residential areas. The architecture of Chan Chan was characterized by its massive adobe brick structures adorned with intricate carvings and friezes depicting geometric patterns, mythological figures, and scenes from daily life

Places

Chan Chan is considered one of the largest pre-Columbian cities in the Americas, covering an area of approximately 20 square kilometers (7.7 square miles). It was built entirely out of adobe, a material made from sun-dried mud bricks. The city was organized into nine major citadels, each with its own compounds, temples, reservoirs, and storage facilities.

Food and Drinks

While Chan Chan was an ancient city and not known specifically for its cuisine, we can infer some traditional foods and drinks that might have been consumed by the people of the Chimú civilization based on archaeological evidence and the culinary practices of the region.
